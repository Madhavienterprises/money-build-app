Basic Option Strategies – Most Popular

1. Covered Call

A Covered Call involves holding a long position in a stock while selling a call option on the same stock. It’s used to generate income on top of stock holdings.

Example: You own 100 shares of Reliance at ₹2400, and you sell a call option with a strike price of ₹2500 expiring in 1 month for ₹50 premium.

2. Protective Put

A Protective Put is used to hedge against potential losses in a stock you already own. You buy a put option to limit downside.

Example: You own TCS at ₹3500 and buy a put option with a strike of ₹3400. If stock falls below ₹3400, the put protects your losses.

3. Long Call

A Long Call is a bullish strategy where you buy a call option to profit from rising stock prices with limited risk.

Example: You buy an Infosys ₹1600 call for ₹30. If Infosys rises above ₹1630, you start making profit.

4. Long Put

A Long Put is used to profit from falling prices. You buy a put option with the expectation that the underlying will drop.

Example: Buy a put option on HDFC Bank ₹1600 strike for ₹40. If HDFC drops below ₹1560, you profit.
